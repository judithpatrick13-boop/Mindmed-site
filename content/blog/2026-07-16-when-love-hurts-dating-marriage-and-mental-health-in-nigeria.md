**"It’s Just Love Wahala" Until It’s Not**

She stopped eating.

He started drinking every weekend.

She checks his phone while he’s in the bathroom.

He can’t sleep without replaying their last fight.




Everyone said, "It’s just relationship wahala. You’ll be fine."

6 months later, both of them were in my DMs asking for a therapist.




In Nigeria, we treat heartbreak like the flu. "Take paracetamol and move on."

We treat marriage problems like "pray about it and manage".

We treat dating stress like "you’re too soft."




But love is not supposed to make you sick.

When your relationship is costing you your peace, your sleep, your appetite, your joy — that’s not just "wahala". That’s your mental health.




Let’s talk about it for real.




## **How Love Starts Hurting Your Mind**

You won’t call it depression. You’ll call it "this guy/girl is giving me issues".




Here’s how it shows up:




#### **1. The Anxiety Loop**

Checking last seen. Re-reading old chats. "Why did he say 'ok' instead of 'okay'?"

Your brain is on 24/7 because you don’t feel safe.




#### **2. The Body Keeps Score**

Migraines after fights. Stomach running. No appetite for days. Or eating everything in the fridge at night.




#### **3. The Isolation**

You stop telling friends what’s happening because "they won’t understand".

You laugh in public, cry in private.




#### **4. The Self-Doubt**

"Am I not enough?"

"Maybe if I were prettier/richer/more submissive..."

You start shrinking yourself to keep the person.




#### **5. The Numbness**

You’re there, but you’re not there. At work, with friends, even with the person.

That’s not "maturity". That’s your mind protecting you.




If 2 or more of these sound like you, love is hurting you right now.




## **Dating in 2026: New App, Same Pain**

Dating didn’t get easier. It just got more confusing.




#### **The Talking Stage Trauma**

3 months of "good morning beautiful" and "I miss you" then... ghosted. No explanation. You’re left with 1000 questions and 0 closure.




#### **The Soft Launch Pressure**

"If you don’t post me, you’re ashamed of me."

"If you post me, my ex will see."

You’re managing a relationship and an audience.




#### **The Timeline Pressure**

"You’re 27. Your mates are married."

"When are you bringing her home?"

Every family gathering becomes an interrogation.




#### **The Comparison Scroll**

His ex looks happier. Her fiancé bought her a car.

You close IG feeling like you’re failing at love.




Dating now feels like a job interview that never ends.  You need therapy because it teaches you: "Their silence is not about your worth."




## **Marriage: Beyond "Just Manage It"**

Marriage in Nigeria is celebrated loudly. The struggle is whispered.




#### **1. Communication Breakdown**

You’re talking, but you’re not hearing each other.

"She nags." "He doesn’t care."

After a while, you both just stop trying.




#### **2. In-Law Wahala**

Your mother is interfering.

Your family expects too much.

Suddenly it’s not 2 people in the marriage. It’s 20.




#### **3. Money Fights**

The #1 fight in Nigerian homes. Especially now.

[Money anxiety ](https://www.mindmed.com.ng/blog/money-anxiety-nigeria/)turns into "You don’t provide" and "You spend too much".

Love gets lost in the spreadsheet.




#### **4. Infertility & Loss**

Months of trying. Negative tests. Miscarriages.

Everyone asks "when are you having kids?" but no one asks "are you okay?"

The grief is silent and heavy.




#### **5. Betrayal & Trust**

Cheating, emotional affairs, lies.

Even if you forgive, your body doesn’t forget. The anxiety stays.




"Staying for the kids" is valid. But "suffering in silence" is not. Couples therapy is for couples who want to fight better, not just stop fighting.




## **When Is It Time to Get Help?**

Ask yourself honestly:




1. Do I feel anxious every time my phone buzzes?

2. Are we having the same fight every 2 weeks with no change?

3. Do I feel lonely even when we’re in the same room?

4. Am I walking on eggshells?

5. Is there yelling, threats, insults, or any form of abuse?

6. Am I staying because "what will people say"?




If you said yes to 2 or more, it’s time.

You can come alone. You can come together. But don’t wait until you’re broken.




## **How Therapy Actually Helps With Love Pain**

A therapist is not here to take sides or break you up.




Here’s what they do:




**For Heartbreak**: Help you grieve without becoming bitter. Teach you how to set boundaries so you don’t go back.




**For Dating**: Help you spot red flags early. Help you stop chasing people who are unavailable.




**For Marriage**: Teach you how to communicate without shouting. Help you and your partner be a team again instead of opponents.




**For Family**: Help you set boundaries with in-laws without starting WW3.




You don’t need to have "big problems" to go to therapy. You just need to want peace.

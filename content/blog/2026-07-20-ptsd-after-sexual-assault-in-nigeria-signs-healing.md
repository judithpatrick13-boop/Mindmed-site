**First, This Is Not Your Fault**

If someone hurt you, I need you to hear this:

It was not your fault.

Not because of what you wore. Not because of where you were. Not because you didn’t fight back or scream or "behave differently."




And what you’re feeling now? The nightmares, the panic, the numbness, the anger?

That has a name. It’s called **PTSD—Post Traumatic Stress Disorder.**




And you’re not crazy for feeling it.

Your mind and body are trying to protect you after something that was never supposed to happen.




You’re not alone in this. And you don’t have to carry it in silence anymore.




## **What PTSD Actually Looks Like After Assault**

PTSD isn’t just "flashbacks in movies". For many Nigerian women and girls, it looks like this:




#### **In Your Body**

\- Heart racing for no reason

\- Trouble sleeping. Nightmares. Waking up at 3am scared

\- Headaches, stomach pain, body pain with no medical cause

\- Jumping when someone touches you unexpectedly




#### **In Your Mind**

\- Replaying what happened, even when you don’t want to

\- Feeling like it’s happening again

\- "What if" thoughts on loop

\- Feeling numb, empty, or like you’re watching your life from outside




#### **In Your Life**

\- Avoiding places, people, songs, or even sex

\- Not trusting anyone. Pushing people away

\- Anger that comes out of nowhere

\- Feeling like you’ll never feel "normal" again

\- Using alcohol, food, or work to shut it all out




If this sounds familiar, your brain is doing what brains do after trauma: it’s trying to keep you safe. But it’s stuck in survival mode.




This can happen whether the assault happened last month or 15 years ago. Whether you were a child or an adult.




## **Why It’s Even Harder in Nigeria**

Healing is hard anywhere. In Nigeria, it comes with extra weight:




#### **1. The Silence**

"Don’t disgrace the family."

"What will people say?"

So you swallow it. And the secret gets heavier.




#### **2. The Blame**

"Why were you out late?"

"Why didn’t you shout?"

"Are you sure it wasn’t your fault?"

That blame doesn’t just come from strangers. Sometimes it comes from family, church, or even the police.




#### **3. The Pressure to "Forgive and Forget"**

Especially in church or community settings. "Just pray about it."

Prayer is good. But prayer and therapy are not enemies. God can heal through people too.




#### **4. No Safe Place to Talk**

Many women don’t report because they don’t trust the system. And they don’t have anyone to talk to who won’t judge them.




So the trauma stays in your body. Unspoken. For years.




##  **"But It Was So Long Ago. Why Am I Still Like This?"**

Because trauma doesn’t have an expiry date.




Maybe you were fine for years. Then you saw someone who looked like them. Or you entered a relationship. Or you had a child. And suddenly everything came back.




That doesn’t mean you’re weak.

That means your body is finally saying: "I need help to process this."




And you deserve that help.

## 
**How Healing Actually Works**

Healing doesn’t mean "forgetting it ever happened".

Healing means it no longer controls you.




Here’s what actually helps:




#### **1. Safety First**

Before anything else, you need to feel safe. In your home, in your body, with the people around you. If you’re still in danger, please reach out to WARIF [0809 210 0009](08092100009) or [Project Alert](https://projectalertnig.org.).




#### **2. Talk to Someone Trained**

A therapist who understands trauma won’t force you to "tell the whole story".

They’ll help you regulate your body first. Then process what happened at your pace.

Therapies like Trauma-Focused CBT and EMDR are proven to help with PTSD. 




#### **3. Grounding When Panic Hits**

Name 5 things you can see. 4 things you can touch. 3 things you can hear.

Cold water on your face. Holding ice.

These bring you back to "I am here. I am safe now."




#### **4. Know That Feelings Come in Waves**

Some days will be okay. Some days will be hard. That’s normal. Healing isn’t linear.




#### **5. You Get to Decide What Healing Looks Like**

Reporting, not reporting. Telling people, not telling.

It’s your choice. And there’s no "right" timeline.

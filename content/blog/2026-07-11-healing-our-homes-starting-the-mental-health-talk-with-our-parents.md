Mommy, Daddy, this isn’t about blaming anyone.  

This is about peace.  




You’ve carried our family through so much. **Grief** after losing loved ones. **Divorce** or separation in the family. **Relocation** to a new city with nothing. **Aging** with body pain and worry. **Parenting** 4, 5, 6 children with little support.  




For years the answer was "just pray", "be strong", "God will do it".  

And you were strong.  




But what if there was more?  

What if therapy wasn’t just for "young people on Twitter"?  

What if it was for you too?  




Let’s talk about it with love, with respect, and without shame.

If this is the first time you’re hearing about it, here’s a simple breakdown of [what to expect on your first therapy](https://www.mindmed.com.ng/blog/First-therapy-session-nigeria-2026/) — no jargon, just truth.




## **Why Is It So Hard For Older Generations?**

Our parents grew up in a time where mental health wasn’t a word. It was called "overthinking" or "spiritual attack".  




1.  "**We didn’t have therapy in our time"** - They survived through community, church, and endurance

2.  **"What will people say?"** - Stigma is real. Reputation matters in our communities

3. ** "Prayer is enough"** - Faith is the foundation. But [therapy and prayer can work together](https://www.mindmed.com.ng/blog/therapy-vs-prayer-vs-friends-nigeria/) — God also gives us doctors and counselors as tools."

4.  **"I’m fine"** - They were taught to swallow pain so the family could move forward




Understanding this helps us talk to them with respect, not arguments.




## **The Things They’re Carrying That No One Talks About**

Therapy isn’t just for "madness". It’s for life. And our parents have lived a lot of life:




\-  **Grief**: Losing a spouse, sibling, or child and never getting to process it. "Just move on" was the advice.

\-   **Divorce/Separation**: The shame, the financial stress, raising kids alone. 

\-   **Relocation**: Moving from the village to Lagos, or Nigeria to abroad. Starting over with no support system.

\-   **Aging**: Chronic pain, retirement fears, loneliness after children leave home.

\-   **Parenting**: The pressure of providing, adult children struggling, and still being expected to "hold the home together".




These things cause anxiety, depression, high BP, and sleepless nights. But they have names now. And there is help.




## **The 5-Step Script: How to Bring It Up With Love**

1.  **Start with their body, not their mind**

    "Dad, I noticed your BP has been high and you’re not sleeping. The doctor said stress from **grief and worry** can do that. There’s someone who helps people manage that stress..."

2.  **Use people they respect**

    "Mama, even Pastor said that mental health is part of health. The church now has counselors because **parenting and life** can be heavy."

3.  **Frame it as strength, not weakness**

    "You’ve carried this family through \*relocation, loss, and everything\*. You deserve someone to help carry you too."

4.  **Offer low-pressure options first**

    "There’s a free 2-minute test online. No one has to know. Let’s just try it together" → [Take the Free MindMed Screening](https://www.mindmed.com.ng/#self-tests)

    Or a free helpline: She Writes Woman 0800 800 2000

5.  **Lead by example**

    "I started therapy and it’s helping me with anxiety. I want the same peace for you."




## **What If They Say No?**

Plant the seed and don’t force it.  

1.  Send them this article on WhatsApp

2.  Leave the free screening link in the family group

3.  Share stories of older Nigerians who got help. Healing is possible at any age.




Remember: Healing one person heals a home.

## FAQ

#### Q: Is therapy only for young people in Nigeria?

A: No. Therapy is for anyone going through stress, grief, or life changes. Many Nigerians 45+ benefit from therapy for aging, parenting, and loss.



#### Q: How do I convince my Nigerian parents to try therapy?

A: Start with their physical health, use people they respect, and offer low-pressure options like a free screening or helpline.



#### Q: Are there free mental health helplines in Nigeria?

A: Yes. She Writes Woman: 0800 800 2000 and SURPIN: 0800 078 7746 are both toll-free and available 24/7.

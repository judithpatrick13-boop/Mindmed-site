“Will the therapist judge me? Do I have to cry? What if I don’t know what to say?” 




If that’s what’s stopping you from booking, you’re not alone. For most Nigerians, fear of the unknown is bigger than the actual problem. In a country where “therapy = madness” is still the stereotype, walking into session 1 feels like walking into the unknown.




Your first therapy session in Nigeria 2026 is nothing like movies. No couch. No forced crying. No diagnosis in the first session.




It’s a 50-minute conversation where you and your therapist figure out 2 things: What’s going on, and what you want to change. You’re in control the whole time.




This guide breaks down exactly what happens, minute by minute, so you can book with less fear + more clarity. 







**Before Session: Booking + Intake Form - 10 mins total**




**Step 1: Book**

Online: Pick your platform and therapist → choose time → pay/get HMO approval → get video chat link.  

In-person: Call clinic → book appointment → get directions. 




**Step 2: Intake form**

You’ll fill 1 page online before session. Nothing scary. Questions like: 

\- What brought you to therapy?

\- Rate your stress/anxiety/sleep on a scale of 1-10

\- Any past therapy/medication?

\- Emergency contact - only used if you’re in danger




**Reality Check:** You can write “Work stress and trouble sleeping” if “depression” feels too heavy. Be honest, but use your words.




Therapist reads this before you meet, so session 1 starts faster. You don’t waste 20 mins explaining basics.




### **Minutes 0-5: The “Hello” Part**




Therapist greets you. Online: “Can you hear me? Are you in a private space?” In-person: Offers water, shows you to the room.




Then they set 3 rules: 

1. Confidentiality → What you say stays here, except if there is risk of suicide, harm to others, or child/elder abuse cases

2. No judgment zone → This is not church, court, or your mom’s parlor 

3. You lead → You decide what to share and when




**What therapist WON’T do**: Stare at you silently, write diagnosis immediately, or ask “so tell me about your childhood” in minute 2.




### **Minutes 5-20: “What Brought You Here?”**




Therapist asks open questions. Examples: 

\- “What made you book this session today?”

\- “When did this start feeling heavy?”

\- “How is this affecting work/relationships/sleep?”




Your job is to talk like you would to a smart friend. No perfect grammar needed. Cry if you need to. Stay quiet if you need to. Therapist will guide you.




**Example:** “Doctor, work stress is killing me. My boss sends emails 11pm. I can’t sleep. My wife says I’m always angry.” That’s enough. The therapist picks it up from there.




You don’t need to “prepare a speech”. Messy + real > perfect + fake.




### **Minutes 20-30: Goals - “What Do You Want to Change?”**




Therapist flips the question: “If therapy works, what would be different in 2 months?”




**Examples:**

\- “I want to sleep without overthinking”

\- “I want to stop snapping at my siblings”

\- “I want to feel confident in meetings again”




**Why this matters:** Therapy without goals = gist session. With goals = progress. Therapist helps make goals small + realistic.




### Minutes 30-40: Therapist Explains Their Approach




**Now your therapist talks. They’ll explain:** 

\- **What they think is happening:** “This sounds like anxiety and burnout”

\- **How they’ll help:** “We’ll use CBT + breathing skills. 6-8 sessions is typical”

\- **Homework:** “Track sleep and  try box breathing 4-4-4-4 breathing when stressed”




**You can ask questions:** “Will I get meds?” “How long will this take?” “What if I miss sessions?” No question is dumb.




### **Minutes 40-50: Plan + Next Steps**




Your Therapist wraps up: 

1. **Summary:** “Today we agreed work stress + poor sleep are main issues. Next session we’ll try thought records.”

2. **Homework:** Usually 1 small task, not an essay. Maybe “5-min walk daily” or “write 3 worries before bed”.

3. **Book next session:** Weekly is standard at start. You can reschedule if Lagos traffic wins.

4. **Crisis plan:** “If you feel suicidal before next session, call 112 or Lagos state 2800 helpline, or go to the nearest hospital if you are unsafe.




You’ll leave with: clarity, 1 tool to try, and date for session 2.




**3 Things Therapists in Nigeria WILL NOT Do in Session 1**




1. **Judge you** → They’ve heard worse. Your story is not “shocking” to them.

2. **Tell your family/boss/pastor** → Confidentiality is law. NPA/MDCN license is at stake.

3. **Force trauma talk on day 1** → “We go at your pace” is rule #1. No one makes you relive pain immediately




**Online vs In-Person First Session: What’s Different?**




**Online - MindMed style:**

**Pros:** No traffic, join from locked room/car, less stigma if neighbors sees you, wear pajamas 😄

**Cons:** Need stable data, private space




**In-person Lagos/Abuja:** 

**Pros:** Face-to-face connection, no network wahala  

**Cons:** Traffic stress, clinic waiting room, someone might see you




**Truth**: Therapy works either way. Pick what makes you comfortable for session 1. Most Nigerians start online, then switch to in-person later if needed.




**Session 1 is Just a Conversation**




No one expects you to have answers. Therapist doesn’t expect you to cry or be “fixed” in 50 mins. 




Session 1 goal is simple: Build trust and make a plan. That’s it.

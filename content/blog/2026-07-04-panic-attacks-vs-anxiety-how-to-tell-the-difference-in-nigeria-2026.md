If your chest tightens, you can’t breathe, and you’re Googling symptoms at 2am, you’re not alone. Most Nigerians mix up “panic attack” and “anxiety”. 

They’re related but different. Knowing which one you have changes how you handle it.

This 2026 guide explains the difference between panic and anxiety in plain Nigerian English. Plus 3 things to do DURING an episode and when to see a therapist.

## Anxiety vs Panic Attack: The 30-Second Difference

**Anxiety** → Builds up slowly like a cloud. “What if I fail the interview? What if I don't make it?  What if…” You worry for hours/days. Your body is tense, but you can still function.

**Panic Attack** → Sudden explosion like thunder. Hits in 10 mins. Heart racing, can’t breathe, feel like you’ll die. Your body screams “DANGER!” but your brain knows nothing is actually wrong.

An example of anxiety: 3 days before salary day, you’re worried about rent. 

An example of a panic attack: You’re at the bus stop, suddenly your chest tightens, sweating, and your thoughts start to spiral, “I’m dying now”.

## 7 Signs You’re Having a Panic Attack, Not Just Anxiety

If 4+ of these hit suddenly + peak in 10 mins = panic attack:

1. **Racing heart/palpitations** → Feels like heart wants to jump out
2. **Can’t breathe/shortness of breath** → Like someone sat on your chest
3. **Dizziness or “about to faint”** → Room spinning
4. **Sweating, shaking, trembling** → Even with AC on
5. **Nausea or stomach upset** → “I want to vomit”
6. **Numbness/tingling** → Hands, feet, face feel weird
7. **Fear of dying/losing control** → “I’m going mad” or “This is a heart attack”

### **Key difference:**

Panic = sudden + intense + physical. 

Anxiety = gradual + mental worry + tension.

The fact is, it’s your body’s alarm system misfiring and clearly a medical condition.

## 3 Things to Do DURING a Panic Attack

**Save this to use next time:**

**1. 5-4-3-2-1 Grounding Technique**

Name 5 things you see, 4 things you touch, 3 things you hear, 2 things you smell, 1 thing you taste. Pulls brain back to NOW. Panic lives in “what if”.

**2. Box Breathing** → Breathe in 4 secs → Hold 4 secs → Out 4 secs → Hold 4 secs. Repeat 4 times. Tells body “we’re safe”. 

**3. Tell yourself the truth** → “This is a panic attack. It’s uncomfortable but not dangerous. It will pass in 10-20 mins. I’ve survived this before.” 

**What NOT to do:** Google symptoms, call 3 people to scream, drink alcohol/energy drink. That makes it worse.

### Anxiety vs Panic: Treatment is Different

**For Anxiety:** Therapy focuses on thought patterns, worry management, and lifestyle. CBT works great. 6-8 sessions.

**For Panic Attacks:** Therapy focuses on “fear of fear” + breathing skills + gradual exposure. Also 6-12 sessions. Sometimes short-term medication from a psychiatrist helps at the start.

## **When to see a doctor/therapist immediately:** 

1. First panic attack ever → Rule out heart/thyroid issues
2. Panic attacks weekly → You deserve relief
3. You’re avoiding places/people because of fear → That’s “agoraphobia” starting

## **Can You Prevent Panic Attacks?**

Yes, with therapy. Therapists teach you to:

1. Spot early warning signs before an explosion
2. Retrain brain, racing heart does not mean you’re dying
3. Reduce overall anxiety so the panic trigger is weaker

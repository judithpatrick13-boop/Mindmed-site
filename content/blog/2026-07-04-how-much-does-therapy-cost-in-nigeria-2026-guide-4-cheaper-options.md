## Therapy Prices in Nigeria 2026

Prices vary by city, format, and therapist experience. Here’s what Nigerians actually pay right now:

#### **Online therapy - Most affordable**

1. **₦3,000 - ₦10,000 per session** → Newer practitioners, 45-50 mins, platforms like MindMed
2. **₦10,000 - ₦15,000 per session** → 5+ years experience, specialists in trauma/OCD/anxiety
3. **₦15,000 - ₦25,000 per session** → Senior psychologists/psychiatrists, 10+ years

#### **In-person therapy** 

1. **₦10,000 - ₦20,000 per session** → Private clinics, mid-level therapists
2. **₦20,000 - ₦50,000+ per session** → Lekki/Victoria Island clinics, senior consultants
3. **₦2,000 - ₦5,000 per session** → Teaching hospitals like LUTH, FMC. Long waitlists though.

One session of online therapy = 2 plates of jollof and chicken in Lagos. One month of weekly therapy = less than your fuel budget for a week.

**Hidden costs to watch**: Some clinics add an “assessment fee” ₦5k-₦10k for the first session. Ask upfront. Good platforms like MindMed show the full price before booking.

## Is Free Therapy Effective? When Free Works vs Paid

Free therapy sounds too good to be true. Here’s the honest breakdown:

Free works for: 

1. Mild stress, overthinking, relationship issues
2. Learning basic coping skills 
3. Deciding if therapy is for you
4. Crisis helplines: Mentally Aware 08008008000, Suicide Prevention 08062102197

Paid therapy is better for:

1. Depression, panic attacks, trauma, OCD
2. You need weekly accountability + structured plan
3. Medication might be needed → only psychiatrists can prescribe
4. You’ve tried free options for 2-3 months but stuck

Start free to test the waters. If after 1 month you’re still struggling daily, invest in paid therapy. Your mind is worth ₦5k/week.

4. Does Health Insurance Cover Therapy in Nigeria? NHIS + HMO Reality 2026

Short answer: Sometimes, but not always.

**NHIS National Health Insurance**: Rarely, but it's changing. Most list “psychiatric care” but exclude talk therapy. You must check your HMO policy.

**Private HMOs:** AXA Mansard/Hygeia started covering in   2024/2025, but only hospital-based. No online therapy covered yet.

**Company health plans:** Big banks, tech companies, Shell, and MTN now include therapy in staff benefits. Ask HR: “Does our HMO cover counseling sessions?”

**How to check:** Call your HMO → “Is outpatient psychotherapy covered? What’s the annual limit?” Get it in writing. If yes, ask for a list of approved therapists.

Reality for 80% of Nigerians is you’ll pay out-of-pocket. That’s why affordable online options matter.

## Common Questions Nigerians Ask About Therapy Cost

**Q: Why is online therapy cheaper?**

A: No office rent, no NEPA bill, therapist sees more clients. You save on transport too. Same license, same quality.

**Q: Is ₦3k/session therapy “low quality”?**

A: No. Newer therapists charge less to build experience. They’re still NPA/MDCN licensed. Check credentials like we taught in “[how to find licensed therapist 2026](https://www.mindmed.com.ng/blog/Find-licensed-therapist-Nigeria-2026-NPA-MDCN-verification/)”.

**Q: How many sessions will I need?**

A: 6-12 sessions for most issues. That’s ₦18k-₦36k total for online. Less than 1 iPhone installment.

**Q: Can I pause therapy if money gets tight?**

A: Yes. Tell your therapist. Good ones will give you coping tools to use until you’re ready to return.

“Ah, it’s not that serious.”  

“Everyone is stressed.”  

“Let me just pray about it.”  




Sound familiar? 




In Nigeria 2026, we’ve mastered the art of explaining away pain. We call anxiety “overthinking”. We call burnout “I’m just tired”. We call depression “spiritual attack” or “phase”.




**You don’t have to be in crisis to need therapy.** You don’t have to be “mad” or “breaking down”. Sometimes the sign is quieter. It’s the weight you’ve been carrying for months, and you've been calling it normal.




If you’ve been asking, “Do I even need therapy?”, this list is for you. 9 signs Nigerian adults ignore until it gets bad.




## **1. You’re Snapping At People You Love For No Reason**




Danfo driver cuts you off. You’re furious for 2 hours.  

Your sister asks, “Have you eaten?” You bite her head off.  

Your partner breathes too loud and you want to fight.




**What’s really happening**: Irritability is anxiety and depression wearing a mask. When your nervous system is overloaded, small things feel huge. 




In Nigeria, we call it “I’m just stressed”. But if everyone around you is walking on eggshells, it’s not just stress. It’s a sign.




## **2. 3am Overthinking That Won’t Shut Up**




You lie down at 11pm. By 3 a.m., you’re replaying that WhatsApp message from 2019.  

“Did I say the wrong thing in that meeting?”  

“What if I lose my job?”  

“What if my child fails WAEC?”




**What’s really happening**: Rumination. Your brain is stuck in a loop trying to “solve” problems at 3am when there’s nothing to solve. Therapy teaches you how to get out of the loop. Prayer helps. Friends help. But CBT gives you tools you can use at 3am.




## **3. “I’m Just Tired”... Every Single Day**




You sleep 8 hours. You still wake up tired.  

Coffee doesn’t help. The weekend doesn’t help.  

Getting out of bed feels like lifting cement.




**What’s really happening**: This is classic burnout and depression fatigue. It’s not laziness. Your brain is using all its energy just to keep you functioning. In Nigeria, we glorify “hustle”. But tiredness that doesn’t go away after rest is your body asking for help.




## **4. You’ve Stopped Doing Things You Used To Love**




You used to love owambe. Now “it’s too much”.  

You used to pray with fire. Now you just watch the service on TV.  

You used to gist with friends. Now you’d rather be alone.




**What’s really happening**: **Anhedonia**. The fancy word for “nothing feels good anymore”. It’s one of the core signs of depression. We explain it away as “I’ve matured” or “I’m focused”. But joy disappearing is not maturity. It’s a symptom.




## **5. You Use “I’m Fine” As A Shield**




“Are you okay?”  

“I’m fine.”  

Inside, you’re drowning.




Nigerians are experts at this. “God dey” while your chest is tight. “All is well” while you haven’t slept in 4 days. 




**What’s really happening**: You’ve learned that vulnerability gets you “pray about it” or “be strong”. So you shut down. Therapy is the one place where “I’m not fine” is safe to say out loud.




## **6. Your Body Is Complaining, But Tests Are Normal**




Headaches that won’t go. Stomach issues. Chest tightness. Back pain.  

The doctor runs tests. “Everything is normal.”




**What’s really happening**: **Psychosomatic symptoms**. Anxiety and depression live in the body, too. In Nigeria, we go to 3 hospitals before we consider therapy. But if your body is screaming and labs are clean, your mind might be the one talking.




## **7. You’re Self-Medicating To Cope**




“Just one bottle to sleep.”  

“Just one joint to calm down.”  

“Just endless scrolling to avoid thinking.”




**What’s really happening**: You found a coping tool. It works short-term. But alcohol, weed, porn, gambling, TikTok for 5 hours = avoidance. Therapy helps you deal with what you’re avoiding, so you don’t need the escape.




## **8. A Major Life Event Shook You, and You Never Processed It**




Job loss. Breakup. Miscarriage. Losing a parent. Japa stress.  

You said, “I’ve moved on”. But 6 months later, you’re still angry/numb/anxious.




**What’s really happening**: Unprocessed grief and trauma don’t disappear. They leak out in other areas. In Nigeria, we’re told to “be strong” and “move on”. Therapy gives you space to actually process, so it stops running your life.




## **9. You’re Asking Google “Do I Need Therapy?”**




This is the biggest one. 




If you’re reading this article, part of you already knows. That quiet voice saying “maybe I should talk to someone” is wisdom, not weakness.




**You don’t need to hit rock bottom to start therapy.** You can go because you want to feel better, not just because you feel terrible.




## **“But What Will People Say?” - The Nigerian Fear**




Let’s address it.  

“People will say I’m mad.” No. Therapy is for sane people dealing with human problems.  

“It’s a white people thing.” 18-25% of Nigerians experience depression/anxiety. This is our problem too.  

“I should just pray more.” Faith and therapy are not enemies. Many pastors and imams now refer members to therapy.




**Therapy in Nigeria 2026 looks like**: 50 minutes on video from your room. No waiting room. No one sees you. Just you and a professional who’s trained to help.

“Just pray about it.”  

“Talk to a friend.”  

“Go for deliverance.”




If you’re struggling in Nigeria, you’ve heard all three before week 1.




And you probably love all three. Prayer gives you peace. Friends make you laugh. Church gives you community.




So where does therapy fit?




Is therapy saying God isn’t enough? Is it saying your friends are useless?  

No.




Think of it like this: Prayer is for your spirit. Friends are for your heart. Therapy is for your mind.




You don’t use paracetamol for a broken leg. You don’t use prayer to file your taxes. Each tool has a job.




Let’s break down what each one actually does so you stop feeling guilty for needing more than one.







## **Talking to Friends: The First Aid**

Your friend is 2am voice notes. “You’re not crazy. I get you.”




#### What friends are great at:

1.  **Immediate comfort**: They’ll send memes, show up with food, and pray with you.

2.  **Shared experience**: “Omo I’ve been there during ASUU strike.” You feel less alone.

3.  **Accountability**: “Girl leave that guy.”




#### **Where friends struggle**:

Friends are biased. They’ll always take your side. They also get tired. You can’t trauma-dump every week or the friendship will burn out.




Most importantly, friends can listen but they can’t teach you how to stop a panic attack at 3am.




If you’ve been feeling off for weeks and talking to friends isn’t helping, it might be time to check if it’s more than “stress”. Take our free 2-minute mental health [self-tests](https://www.mindmed.com.ng/#self-tests) to get clarity before it gets worse.




Friends are like band-aids. Essential. But band-aid doesn’t fix the wound underneath.










## **Prayer and Faith: The Foundation**

For 90% of Nigerians, faith is everything. And it should be.




#### **What prayer and church are great at:**

1.  **Meaning and Hope**: “God is with me in this.” That keeps people alive.

2.  **Community**: Church, mosque, fellowship. You’re not doing life alone.

3.  **Forgiveness and Peace**: Guilt and shame melt when you surrender.




Many therapists in Nigeria are Christian and Muslim. We’ve had sessions that started with “Can we pray first?” Faith and therapy are not enemies.




#### **Where faith alone struggles**:

Clinical depression is not a “lack of faith”. Jesus wept. Elijah was depressed. David wrote Psalms from dark places.  

If you have a chemical imbalance, trauma, or panic disorder, prayer helps your spirit but your brain may still need tools and treatment.  

God gave us doctors for malaria. He also gave us therapists for the mind.










## **Therapy: The Training Gym for Your Mind**

This is the part people fear most. “So you want me to talk to a stranger?”




#### **What therapy is actually**:

A professional teaching you skills. Like a gym coach, but for your thoughts and emotions.




#### **What therapists are great at**:

**1.  No judgment, Confidentiality**: NDPR law protects you. What you say stays in the room. Not on WhatsApp.

**2.  Tools, not just talk**: Therapists teach you how to stop anxiety spirals, set boundaries with toxic parents, handle anger without shouting.

**3.  Diagnosis and Treatment**: Is this burnout or depression? Is this grief or trauma? Therapists are trained to tell the difference.




## **What therapy is NOT:**

Not “white people nonsense”. Not replacing God. Not sitting in silence for 1 hour.




It’s work. But it works.




If you’re not sure whether what you’re feeling needs a therapist or just rest, this post on [9 Signs You Actually Need Therapy](https://www.mindmed.com.ng/blog/signs-you-need-therapy-nigeria-2026/) will help you decide without the guilt.







**So How Do You Choose?**

You don’t have to pick one. The healthiest Nigerians use all three.




**If it’s a bad day, heartbreak, or stress**: Start with prayer and your friend. Vent. Cry. Laugh.




**If it’s been 2 weeks and you still can’t function**: Add therapy. That’s when talking + praying needs backup with actual tools.




**If it’s panic attacks, trauma, or you can’t sleep**: Start with therapy. Then bring in prayer and your support system to help you heal.




**If you just want to grow**: Therapy helps you understand yourself. Prayer gives you direction. Friends keep you grounded.




Pray for strength. Talk to your friend. And go to therapy to learn how to use that strength.







#### **“But Is Therapy Against My Faith?”**

Short answer: No.




At MindMed, we’ve worked with believers. Therapy doesn’t argue with your beliefs. It asks: “Given what you believe, how can we help you live better, with less pain?”




God heals. Sometimes through prayer. Sometimes through people. Sometimes through professionals He trained.







You’re not weak for needing therapy. You’re wise.




Nehemiah prayed AND built the wall. David prayed AND went to war with strategy. You can pray AND go to therapy.

If you've been putting off therapy in Lagos because you're not sure what it costs, you're not alone. Pricing in Nigeria's mental health space is scattered across private clinics, online platforms, university clinics, and public hospitals — and very few of them publish their rates anywhere you can actually find them. This guide pulls together real, current price ranges so you can budget for care before you ever pick up the phone.

## What does therapy cost in Lagos right now?

Across Lagos, a single therapy session typically falls between **₦15,000 and ₦34,500**, with the full market ranging from about ₦8,000 on the low end to ₦80,500 at the most exclusive private practices. Where you land in that range depends mostly on three things: the type of professional you see, where their practice is based, and whether you're paying out of pocket or through insurance.

### By provider type

- **Clinical psychologists:** roughly ₦18,000–₦51,750 per session, averaging around ₦30,000
- **Psychiatrists:** roughly ₦25,000–₦80,500 per session, averaging around ₦40,000 (psychiatrists can prescribe medication, which is part of why rates run higher)
- **Counsellors and general therapists:** generally the most affordable starting point, often below ₦15,000

### By location within Lagos

Where a clinic sits on the map makes a real difference:

- **Ikoyi / Banana Island:** ₦25,000–₦80,500 (40–100% above the city average)
- **Victoria Island / Lekki Phase 1:** ₦22,000–₦69,000 (30–80% above average)
- **Ajah / Lekki Peninsula:** ₦18,000–₦51,750 (10–40% above average)
- **Mainland areas (Yaba, Surulere, Ikeja):** generally closest to the city average, often the most budget-friendly for in-person care

## Lower-cost and free options

If the numbers above feel out of reach, there are real, legitimate options that cost far less — some entirely free:

- **University counselling centres:** Lagos State University charges around ₦4,000–₦11,500 per session; Pan-Atlantic University runs ₦6,000–₦17,250. These are generally open to students only.
- **Public teaching hospitals:** Lagos University Teaching Hospital (LUTH) and Lagos State University Teaching Hospital (LASUTH) offer sessions from around ₦2,500–₦11,500 depending on the service.
- **Federal Neuro-Psychiatric Hospital, Yaba:** one of the most affordable clinical options in the city, from roughly ₦2,000–₦9,200.
- **Nonprofits and income-based programs:** Mentally Aware Nigeria Initiative (MANI) prices sessions from ₦3,000–₦11,500 based on what you can afford, and She Writes Woman offers support ranging from free to about ₦9,200, with a focus on women.
- **Online platforms:** Lagos-based online therapists typically charge ₦12,000–₦28,750 per session, though some platforms advertise entry-level sessions from as low as ₦3,000–₦8,000. Online sessions also save you the cost and time of Lagos traffic, which is worth factoring in.

## Does insurance or NHIS cover it?

Coverage exists, but it's limited. Most private health plans that include mental health benefits cap coverage at around **5–8 sessions per year**, with copays typically running ₦5,000–₦11,500 per visit. The National Health Insurance Scheme has historically offered very limited mental health coverage compared to general medical care, so most people budget for therapy as an out-of-pocket cost rather than relying on insurance to cover it. If you do have an HMO plan, it's worth calling them directly before booking, since coverage details vary a lot by provider and plan tier.

## Sliding scale: the option many people don't know about

A number of Lagos therapists offer **sliding-scale pricing** — a reduced rate based on your income — typically landing between ₦5,000 and ₦13,800 per session. This isn't widely advertised, so the simplest approach is to just ask directly when you reach out: "Do you offer a sliding scale, and what's the lowest rate available?" Most ethical providers will tell you plainly, even if the answer is no.

## FAQ

**Is therapy in Lagos covered by NHIS?**
Coverage is limited. NHIS and most private HMOs offer minimal mental health benefits compared to general healthcare, and where coverage exists it's usually capped at a small number of sessions per year with a copay. Always confirm directly with your specific plan before assuming a session will be covered.

**What's the cheapest way to access therapy in Lagos?**
Public teaching hospitals (LUTH, LASUTH) and the Federal Neuro-Psychiatric Hospital in Yaba offer some of the lowest rates in the city, often under ₦10,000 per session. Nonprofits like MANI and She Writes Woman also offer income-based or free support, and university students have access to discounted counselling centres on campus.

**Is online therapy cheaper than in-person therapy in Lagos?**
Usually, yes. Online sessions remove the overhead of a physical office and the need to factor in transport, which means many online providers can charge less than equivalent in-person sessions — plus you skip Lagos traffic entirely.

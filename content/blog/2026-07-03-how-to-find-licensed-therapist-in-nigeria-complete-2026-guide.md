## What “Licensed Therapist” Actually Means in Nigeria

Licensed = Government approved + Trained + Accountable

In Nigeria, legit mental health professionals fall under 3 main categories:

**1. Clinical Psychologist** → 6+ years training. Licensed by the Nigerian Psychological Association NPA. Handles therapy for anxiety, depression, trauma, etc.  

**2. Psychiatrist** → Medical doctor + 5-6 years training + residency. Licensed by the Medical & Dental Council of Nigeria, MDCN. Can prescribe medication if needed.  

**3. Counseling Psychologist/Therapist →** Master’s in counseling psychology. Registered with Association of Professional Counsellors of Nigeria APCON or similar bodies. Focuses on talk therapy, relationships, career stress.

Aunty A who says “I’m a counselor because I give advice” is not a licensed therapist. Aunty B who studied psychology and has an NPA license = licensed therapist.

**Red flag:** “Life coach”, “Motivational speaker”, “Relationship expert” with no degree. They can motivate you, but they’re not trained to treat clinical mental health conditions. If you have panic attacks or depression, you need licensed help.

## 3 Quick Ways to Verify a Therapist’s License

Don’t feel shy about asking. Real therapists expect this. If they’re offended, that’s a red flag.

**1. Ask for license number directly** → “What’s your NPA or MDCN license number?” Write it down. Licensed professionals are proud of their training and will share it.

**2. Check the body’s website**

\- For psychologists: Go to http://npanigeria.org → “Find a Psychologist”. Search their name + state.  

\- For psychiatrists: Go to http://mdcn.gov.ng → “Medical Register”. Search name.  

Takes 2 minutes. If the name + number don’t match, walk away.

**3.Look for degree + training proof** → Minimum BSc Psychology for counselors. Masters/PhD for clinical psychologists. Ask “Where did you train?” Then Google the school. Fake therapists hate specific questions.

“But won’t they think I don’t trust them?” No. Good therapists want you to verify. It means you’re serious about your health. Fraudsters are the ones who get angry.

## **Online vs In-Person: Which is Safer in Nigeria?**

Online therapy is legit in Nigeria since COVID. Same license, same training, same confidentiality rules. Just a Zoom/WhatsApp call instead of the office.

**Why online wins for Nigerians:**

1. No traffic → 3 hours in Lagos traffic vs 1-hour session from your room  
2. Privacy → No “who saw me enter the clinic” from neighbors  
3. Flexibility → Book 9pm sessions after work. No need to take leave  
4. Cost → Often 20-30% cheaper than in-person Lagos rates

## Checklist for safe online therapy:

1. Video call option available, not just chat  
2. Clear privacy policy on website  
3. Licensed therapist profile you can verify on NPA/MDCN  
4. Secure payment + session notes stored safely

In-person wins if you need psychiatric medication management, you prefer face-to-face, or you don’t have stable internet.

## Red Flags: How to Spot Fake “Therapists”

Protect yourself. Walk away if you see these:

1. No license + gets angry when asked → “Do you trust me or not?”  
2. Guarantees cure in 1-3 sessions → Real therapy takes time. No honest therapist promises quick fixes.  
3. Tells you what to do with your life → “Leave your husband now”. Good therapy helps YOU decide.  
4. No confidentiality → Shares other clients’ stories with you. They’ll share yours too.  
5. Only chats on WhatsApp, no video, no profile → Too anonymous. No accountability.

If it feels off in session 1, it probably is. You’re allowed to switch therapists. It’s not rude. It’s self-care.

## What to Do Before Your First Session

Preparation = better results + less anxiety.

1. **Check your reason** → “Anxiety”, “Stress”, “Relationship issues”, “Grief”. Be honest, even if it sounds “small”.  
2. **Write 3 things you want help with** → “I can’t sleep”, “I overthink”, “I panic in crowds”. Saves time in session 1.  
3. **Ask about fees upfront** → “How much per session? Any package deals?” No surprise charges. Next post covers therapy cost Nigeria 2026 in detail.  
4. **Trust your gut** → First 10 mins: Do you feel safe + heard? Does the therapist listen or just talk? If no, switch. It’s normal.  
5. **Set up your space** → For online: quiet room, earphones, water. Turn off notifications. This is your time.

You wake up with your heart racing. Deadline is 10am. Your boss just dropped “urgent” on the group chat. Is this stress… or anxiety?

For many Nigerians, the line is blurry. We say “I’m stressed” when we actually mean “I’m anxious”. We say “I’ll be fine” even though our bodies have been in panic mode for months.

## Here’s the truth: Stress has a trigger; Anxiety doesn’t.

Stress shows when there is no electricity, your generator needs a plug, and your laptop is at 3%. Anxiety is when the light is back, work is done, but your chest still feels tight at 2am.

In the next 5 minutes, you’ll learn **5 key differences** between anxiety and stress that Nigerians often ignore. And by the end, you’ll know which one fits you + what to do next.

### Stress has a trigger. Anxiety doesn’t

**Stress is a response.** Something happens → you react—deadline, traffic on your way to an interview, rent due next week. Remove the trigger, stress drops.

**Anxiety is a state**. Nothing “bad” is happening right now, but your brain still scans for danger. You’re in church, work is fine, family is healthy… yet you can’t relax. 

Stress = “I’m worried about this client presentation tomorrow”. Anxiety = “What if I fail every presentation for the rest of my life?”

### Stress fades when the problem is solved

**Stress is short-term.** Submit the report, get your salary, fix the generator → your body calms down. It’s your body’s alarm system doing its job.

**Anxiety lingers.** The exam ended 2 weeks ago, but you’re still replaying it. You got the job, but now you’re worried about losing it. **Your body stays in panic mode** even when there’s no fire.

**Red flag:** If you’ve felt “on edge” for **6+ months** with no clear reason, that’s not just stress.

### Anxiety affects your sleep even on “good” days

Both can mess with sleep. But the pattern is different.

**Stress sleep:** You can’t sleep because you’re thinking about tomorrow’s problem. Solution comes → you sleep fine again.

**Anxiety sleep:** You wake up at 3am with your heart pounding. No dream, no reason. Or you sleep 10 hours and still feel drained. Anxiety lives in your body, not just your thoughts.

**Reality:** “I don’t have time to be anxious” - but your body is tired all the time anyway. That’s anxiety wearing a “hustle” mask.

### Stress feels like pressure. Anxiety feels like danger

**Stress**: “Too much on my plate”. Tight chest, clenched jaw, irritability. You know why you feel this way.

**Anxiety**: “Something terrible is about to happen”. Dizziness, stomach issues, constant worry, fear of losing control. Your brain screams danger even when you’re safe. 

**Quick test:** Can you name the reason you feel bad? If yes = stress. If your answer is “I don’t know, I just feel off” = anxiety.

Stress is short-term. Anxiety rewires your body

**Stress comes and goes.** It’s a season. NYSC, wedding planning, new baby, relocation. It’s hard, but it ends.

**Anxiety, if ignored, becomes your default setting**. Your brain gets used to panic. **Small things trigger big reactions.** You start avoiding people, work, and decisions. This is when it affects your job, relationships, and health. My sister thought she was just stressed for 6 months until she talked to a therapist.

**Myth:** Anxiety is for “weak people”.

**Fact:** Anxiety is your brain trying to protect you… But it’s overprotective. It can happen to anyone. Old, young, rich, poor.

## Which One Do You Have?

#### Use this 3-question check:

1. Can I point to what’s causing this feeling right now? 
2. Will I feel better once that thing is done?
3. Do I feel safe in my body most days?

If you said “No” to **2+ questions,** look closer at anxiety. Not to diagnose yourself, but to get clarity.

Stress is your body saying, “This is a lot right now”. Anxiety is your body saying, “I don’t feel safe, even now”.

Here are 9 free options you can access today from anywhere in Nigeria:







## **1. Free Hotlines: Talk to Someone Right Now**

When it’s urgent, and you need a human voice. All of these are toll-free or low-cost and run 24/7.




#### **1. SURPIN - Suicide Research and Prevention Initiative**

**Toll-Free**: 0800 078 7746

**Other Lines**: 0903 440 0009 (MTN), 0908 021 7555 (9mobile)

**Languages**: English, Pidgin, Hausa

Nigeria’s most structured suicide prevention helpline. Trained counselors for crisis, depression, and referrals. Available 24/7 




#### **2. Lagos Lifeline - Lagos State Government**

**Number**: 0909 000 MIND / 0909 000 6463

Free psychosocial support, counselling and psychotherapy for Lagos residents. Covers suicide, domestic violence, and general mental health




#### **3. She Writes Woman - Toll-Free Helplin**e

**Number**: 0800 800 2000

No airtime needed. Licensed counselors provide on-the-spot support. Created for anxiety, grief, and emotional distress




#### **4. National Emergency Hotline**

**Number**: 112

For life-threatening emergencies. Toll-free, 24/7 




**Pro tip:** Save 1 number in your phone now. When anxiety hits, you won’t be searching.

## **3. Free Support + Learning: You’re Not Alone**

Healing happens faster when people get it.




#### **8. She Writes Woman + MANI Online Communities**

**Where**: Facebook and Instagram

**What**: Private, moderated groups where Nigerians talk about depression, postpartum, grief, and relationships. No trolling. MANI also runs peer support on social media 24/7

## **“But Are Free Resources Enough?”**

Here’s the honest truth:




#### **Free resources are perfect for:**

1. Crisis moments — call SURPIN 0800 078 7746 or Lagos Lifeline

2. Learning coping skills — use Fade AI and Insight Timer

3. Feeling less alone — join She Writes Woman community

4. Getting clarity — take the [MindMed Free Screening](https://www.mindmed.com.ng/#)




#### **When to upgrade to therapy:**

1. It’s been 2+ weeks and you still can’t function

2. You have panic attacks, trauma, or thoughts of harming yourself

3. You need someone to hold you accountable and give you a custom plan




Think of free resources as first aid. Therapy is physical therapy after the injury. Both matter.
